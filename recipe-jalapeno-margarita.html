Jalapeño Watermelon Margarita

July 14, 2020  ·  by Yuly

Y'all, this is THE summer cocktail. Sweet, juicy watermelon meets the kick of fresh jalapeño in a margarita that is absolutely addictive. Once you try this, basic margaritas will never hit the same. 🍉🌶️

This recipe is super easy to make and comes together in minutes. The key is using fresh watermelon — not juice from a bottle. The freshness makes ALL the difference.

Ingredients (makes 2 cocktails):

  • 3 cups fresh watermelon, cubed and seeds removed
  • 2 oz silver tequila (per drink)
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice (per drink)
  • ½ oz triple sec or Cointreau (per drink)
  • 1–2 jalapeño slices, seeds removed (adjust to your heat preference)
  • Tajín or chili-lime salt for the rim
  • Ice
  • Lime wedges and jalapeño slices for garnish

Directions:

  1. Run a lime wedge around the rim of your glasses and dip into Tajín or chili-lime salt. Set aside.
  2. Add watermelon cubes to a blender and blend until smooth. Strain through a fine mesh strainer to remove any pulp. You should get about 1 cup of fresh watermelon juice.
  3. In a cocktail shaker, muddle 1–2 jalapeño slices (no seeds) to release the heat and flavor. The longer you muddle, the spicier it gets — you've been warned!
  4. Add the tequila, fresh lime juice, triple sec, and watermelon juice to the shaker. Fill with ice.
  5. Shake vigorously for 15–20 seconds until very cold.
  6. Double strain into your prepared glasses over fresh ice to catch any jalapeño bits.
  7. Garnish with a fresh jalapeño slice and a lime wheel. Drink immediately and make more. You'll need them.

Pro tips: If you want less heat, remove all seeds from the jalapeño and only muddle briefly. If you want it spicier, leave some seeds in and muddle longer. You can also infuse the tequila with jalapeño overnight for an even deeper heat. Make it a pitcher for parties — just multiply and keep in the fridge. Add ice per glass when serving so it doesn't get watered down.
