Grilled Peaches with Burrata & Prosciutto

July 9, 2020  ·  by Yuly

This dish right here is the definition of summer on a plate. Grilled peaches, creamy burrata, salty prosciutto, fresh arugula, and a drizzle of honey — it is stunning and takes about 10 minutes to make. Guests will think you went to culinary school. (I mean, I did, but still.)

Use ripe but firm peaches so they hold up on the grill without turning to mush. The grill marks give them a subtle smokiness that pairs insanely well with the cold, creamy burrata. This is a no-cook-level effort with a 10/10 presentation payoff.

Ingredients (serves 4 as appetizer):

  • 3 ripe but firm peaches, halved and pitted
  • 8 oz fresh burrata cheese (2 balls)
  • 4–6 slices prosciutto di Parma
  • 2 cups baby arugula
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for brushing + dressing)
  • 1 tbsp balsamic glaze
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Flaky sea salt & cracked black pepper
  • Crushed pistachios (optional but highly recommended)

Directions:

  1. Heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the cut sides of the peaches with olive oil and a tiny pinch of salt.
  2. Place the peaches cut-side down on the grill. Cook 3–4 minutes without moving, until you have clear grill marks and the peaches have softened slightly. Flip and cook 1–2 minutes on the skin side. Remove and let cool slightly.
  3. On a large serving platter or board, arrange the arugula as the base. Drizzle lightly with olive oil and a pinch of salt — toss gently.
  4. Nestle the grilled peach halves among the arugula, cut-side up. Tear the prosciutto into rustic pieces and drape over and around the peaches.
  5. Break open the burrata balls and place them on the platter — the creamy center will spill out beautifully. Don't stress about making it look perfect; rustic is the vibe here.
  6. Drizzle generously with honey and balsamic glaze over the entire platter.
  7. Finish with fresh basil leaves, crushed pistachios if using, a good crack of black pepper, and a few flakes of sea salt.
  8. Serve immediately with crusty bread or crostini to scoop up all that creamy burrata goodness.

Pro tips: Don't skip the flaky salt at the end — it cuts through the sweetness and richness perfectly. If you can't find burrata, fresh mozzarella works, but burrata is worth hunting down. For a sweeter, more caramelized peach, brush with a tiny bit of honey before grilling. This also works beautifully with nectarines.
