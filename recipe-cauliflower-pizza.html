Cauliflower Crust Pizza

May 11, 2020  ·  by Yuly

Cauliflower Crust Pizza is life changing 🙌 #RealTalk. This is one of the easiest and most delicious pizzas I've made at home — and it's low carb, fam! It's a triple winner recipe. Healthy, easy and delicious!

Ingredients:

  • 1 head cauliflower
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • ½ cup parmesan cheese, freshly grated
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Toppings:

  • ½ cup pizza sauce
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Pepperoni slices (or your favorite toppings)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Parmesan for finishing
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. In a large skillet or pot, bring water to a boil — enough water to cover cauli florets. Season water with salt. Roughly chop cauliflower in even pieces and add to the water in one even layer. Cook for 3 minutes until crisp-tender. Transfer to a dish with paper towel and squeeze to drain water.
  2. Add drained cauli to food processor and pulse until grated. You want to get a cauliflower-rice-like texture — not too fine, not too chunky. Work in batches depending on the size of your food processor.
  3. Transfer riced cauliflower to a clean dish towel or cheesecloth and squeeze out as much moisture as possible. This step is critical — the drier the cauliflower, the crispier your crust will be.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the squeezed cauliflower, egg, mozzarella, parmesan, oregano, garlic powder, salt and pepper. Mix until everything is well combined and forms a dough-like mixture.
  5. Line a baking sheet or pizza pan with parchment paper and lightly spray with cooking spray. Transfer the cauliflower mixture to the center and press out into a round pizza shape, about ¼ inch thick. Keep the edges slightly thicker to form a crust.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and starting to crisp on the edges. Keep an eye on it — every oven is different!
  7. Remove from oven, top with pizza sauce, shredded mozzarella, pepperoni and any other toppings you love. Return to oven for 10–12 more minutes until cheese is bubbly and toppings are cooked through.
  8. Finish with fresh basil, grated parmesan and red pepper flakes. Let cool for 2–3 minutes before slicing. Enjoy!

Pro tips: The secret to a crispy crust is removing as much water as possible from the cauliflower. Don't skip the squeezing step! Also, make sure your oven is fully preheated before putting the crust in. The high initial heat helps set the crust so it holds its shape when you add toppings.

You can swap pepperoni for any toppings you love — roasted veggies, prosciutto, mushrooms, or go fully veggie. The crust is the star, so anything works on top!
