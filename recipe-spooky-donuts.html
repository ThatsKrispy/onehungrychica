Spooky Donuts

October 31, 2023  ·  by Yuly

BOO! 👻 These Spooky Donuts are here to haunt your Halloween spread in the best way possible. We're talking fluffy baked donuts dressed up in black and orange glaze with all the creepy toppings — spider webs, ghost faces, candy eyes, the works. They're a hit with kids AND adults, and way easier than they look.

The base is a classic baked donut — not fried! — so they come together quickly and the kitchen stays clean. You can go as wild or as simple as you want with the decorations. I went full Halloween chaos and I regret nothing.

Ingredients (makes 12 donuts):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup buttermilk
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract

For the glazes & decorating:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 4–6 tbsp milk or heavy cream
  • Orange gel food coloring
  • Black gel food coloring
  • Candy eyeballs
  • Black and orange sprinkles
  • White chocolate melts (for web drizzle)
  • Halloween themed cookie cutouts or fondant shapes (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease a donut pan well with nonstick spray — get into all the crevices.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and sugar.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, buttermilk, melted butter, and vanilla. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir until just combined — do not overmix. A few lumps are fine and expected.
  4. Transfer batter to a large zip-lock bag or piping bag. Snip the corner and pipe batter into the donut pan, filling each cavity about ¾ full.
  5. Bake 10–12 minutes until a toothpick inserted comes out clean and the tops spring back when lightly pressed. Cool in pan 5 minutes then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely — do not glaze warm donuts!
  6. Make your glazes: divide powdered sugar and milk into two bowls. Color one orange and one black using gel food coloring. Stir until smooth and drizzle-able. If too thick, add milk 1 tsp at a time.
  7. Dip the top of each donut into your chosen glaze color. Let the excess drip off. Place back on the wire rack over a sheet of parchment to catch drips.
  8. While glaze is still wet, add decorations: press in candy eyeballs, sprinkle on sprinkles, drizzle white chocolate in a spider web pattern, add fondant ghosts or spiders. Get creative — there are no rules on Halloween!
  9. Let glaze set completely (about 20 minutes) before serving. Display on a spooky platter and watch them disappear.

Pro tips: Gel food coloring gives a much more vibrant color than liquid food coloring — especially for getting true black. Don't cheap out on the candy eyeballs; they make the donuts look amazing with minimal effort. These are best eaten the same day but will keep in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.
