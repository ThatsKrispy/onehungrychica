Skillet Jerk-Butter Shrimp

May 18, 2020  ·  by Yuly

15 minutes. One pan. Maximum flavor. This Skillet Jerk-Butter Shrimp is everything — bold jerk seasoning, rich browned butter, garlic, and plump shrimp all come together in a sauce that you will absolutely mop up with bread. This one's a weeknight staple in my kitchen.

The key is not overcooking the shrimp. They go from perfect to rubbery in about 30 seconds, so stay at the stove and keep your eyes on them. You want them just pink and curled — then pull them off the heat immediately.

Ingredients (serves 2–3):

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails on or off — your call)
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp jerk seasoning (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1 tsp dried)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh scallions and parsley for garnish
  • Crusty bread or rice for serving

Directions:

  1. Pat shrimp dry with paper towels — this is critical for getting a good sear instead of steaming. Season with jerk seasoning, smoked paprika, cayenne, and a pinch of salt. Toss to coat evenly.
  2. Heat a large cast iron or stainless skillet over high heat until very hot (2–3 minutes). Add 1 tbsp of butter and let it melt and begin to brown — don't let it burn.
  3. Add the shrimp in a single layer. Do not crowd the pan — work in batches if needed. Cook 1–1½ minutes per side until pink and slightly caramelized. Remove shrimp and set aside.
  4. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 2 tbsp butter to the same pan. Once melted and beginning to brown, add garlic and thyme. Cook 60 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant and golden (not burnt).
  5. Squeeze in lime juice — it will sizzle and deglaze the pan. Stir and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom (that's flavor). Cook 30 seconds.
  6. Return shrimp to the pan and toss to coat in the garlic-jerk-butter sauce. Cook 30 more seconds just to rewarm. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  7. Transfer to a serving dish. Garnish with sliced scallions and fresh parsley. Serve immediately with crusty bread to dip into the sauce — don't let that sauce go to waste!

Pro tips: Use fresh lime — not bottled. The acidity brightens everything up. If your jerk seasoning has salt, go easy on adding extra. Brown butter (not just melted butter) adds a nutty depth that really elevates this dish — watch it closely so it doesn't burn. This sauce is also incredible over pasta or polenta.
